# Guild of Systems Weavers

> Systems Weavers are practitioners who connect systems, teams, data, workflows and institutional realities across boundaries where formal ownership is fragmented or absent.

The Guild of Systems Weavers exists to recognise, develop and advocate for the practice of cross-system infrastructure work: the skilled labour of joining technologies, teams, workflows, data and institutional realities into functioning services.

We value practical synthesis over silo purity, working systems over ornamental diagrams, and the often invisible craft of keeping complexity connected.

---

## Why this guild exists

Many infrastructure and transformation projects depend on people who work across technical, organisational, and procedural boundaries. These practitioners — often described informally as "glue people", "plumbers", or "Swiss army knives" — hold systems together in ways that are routinely relied upon but rarely recognised.

This guild provides:

### Recognition

Language and legitimacy for cross-boundary infrastructure practice. The work of connecting systems across messy organisational boundaries is real, skilled, and consequential — and it deserves a name.

### Codification

Shared patterns, methods, and frameworks that make tacit expertise visible and transferable. What experienced practitioners carry in their heads can be documented, discussed, and built upon.

### Professionalisation

Contribution-based standing that is portable across employers, sectors, and career paths. Your expertise belongs to you, not to any single organisation.
