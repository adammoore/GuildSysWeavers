## Purpose

The Guild of Systems Weavers exists to:

1. **Recognise** cross-boundary infrastructure practice as a distinct and skilled professional discipline
2. **Codify** the tacit knowledge of practitioners into shared patterns, frameworks, and methods
3. **Support** practitioners in demonstrating competence and contribution through peer recognition
4. **Maintain** an independent, open, professional commons for this emerging field

## Scope

The guild is concerned with the practice of connecting systems, teams, data, workflows, and institutional realities across boundaries where formal ownership is fragmented or absent.

This includes work in:

- IT infrastructure and enterprise services
- Digital transformation and operational change
- Systems integration and interoperability
- Research infrastructure and data management
- Service design across organisational boundaries
- Any domain where cross-system coherence depends on skilled human practice

The guild is **not** concerned with:

- Promoting any particular technology, vendor, or methodology
- Replacing existing professional bodies or certifications
- Creating a gatekeeping mechanism for employment

## Principles

### Independence

The guild is not owned by or aligned with any employer, vendor, or institution. It is a professional commons that belongs to its practitioners.

### Practice over theory

The guild values demonstrated practice and contribution over abstract credentials. Knowledge should be grounded in real operational experience.

### Openness

All core knowledge resources — patterns, frameworks, and methods — are publicly available. The guild builds shared understanding, not proprietary advantage.

### Peer recognition

Standing in the guild is earned through contribution and peer acknowledgement, not through payment, examination, or institutional endorsement.

### Respect for practitioners

The guild honours the full range of career paths that produce cross-boundary expertise, including non-linear, contract-based, cross-sector, and otherwise unconventional professional histories.

### Responsible practice

Members are expected to work ethically and to consider the broader human and institutional consequences of their interventions. See the [Code of Practice](code-of-practice).

## Governance

The guild is currently in its founding phase. Governance is deliberately lightweight and will evolve as the community grows.

### Current model

- The founding maintainers steward the repository and public resources
- Contributions are accepted through pull requests and reviewed by maintainers
- Significant decisions about scope, structure, or direction are discussed in issues before being acted upon
- The [governance notes](governance/) document ongoing decisions and their rationale

### Future evolution

As the community grows, governance will be developed through open discussion. Possible future structures include:

- A stewardship group drawn from active contributors
- Formal decision-making processes for significant changes
- Working groups for specific domains (patterns, competencies, governance)

The guild's governance should remain as simple as possible for as long as possible, adding structure only when it is clearly needed.
