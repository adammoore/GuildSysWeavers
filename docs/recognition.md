The guild uses a lightweight, contribution-based model for acknowledging practitioners' standing. This is peer recognition, not accreditation — it reflects demonstrated participation and contribution to the shared knowledge commons, not a qualification to practise.

## Principles

- Recognition is **earned through contribution**, not through payment or examination
- It reflects **what you have shared**, not what you claim to know
- It is **granted by peers**, not by a central authority
- It is designed to be **portable** — it belongs to you, not to any employer

## Levels

### Contributor

Anyone who has made a meaningful contribution to the guild's knowledge commons.

**How to reach this level:**
- Submit a pattern, case study, glossary entry, or other content that is accepted into the repository
- Provide substantive review or improvement of existing content
- Contribute to governance or community discussions in ways that advance the guild's work

### Practitioner

A contributor who has demonstrated sustained engagement and practice-grounded knowledge.

**How to reach this level:**
- Multiple accepted contributions that show breadth or depth of practice experience
- Contributions that reflect direct operational experience, not only theoretical knowledge
- Active participation in review, discussion, and refinement of guild resources

### Senior Practitioner

A practitioner who has made significant contributions to the guild's body of knowledge and who actively supports other contributors.

**How to reach this level:**
- Substantial body of contributed patterns, case studies, or framework content
- Demonstrated mentoring or support of other contributors
- Contributions that have notably advanced the guild's knowledge or practice

### Pattern Steward

A senior practitioner who takes responsibility for maintaining and developing a domain of the guild's knowledge commons.

**How to reach this level:**
- Sustained stewardship of a section of the pattern library, competency framework, or other core resource
- Active curation — reviewing new contributions, maintaining coherence, identifying gaps
- Recognised by peers as a reliable authority in their stewardship domain

### Fellow

A practitioner whose contributions have been foundational or transformative to the guild or to the broader recognition of this craft.

**How to reach this level:**
- Exceptional and sustained contribution to the guild's mission
- Nomination and endorsement by existing Fellows and Pattern Stewards
- Work that has significantly advanced the field's visibility, knowledge base, or professional standing

## How recognition works

Recognition is currently managed through the guild's repository:

1. **Contributions** are tracked through pull requests and issues
2. **Nominations** for advancement are made through issues using the appropriate template
3. **Decisions** are made by existing recognised members at or above the relevant level
4. **Recognition** is recorded in the contributor's profile in the repository

As the guild grows, this process will be formalised further — but it should always remain contribution-based, peer-driven, and as simple as possible.

## What recognition is not

- It is **not a professional certification** and should not be presented as one
- It is **not a guarantee of competence** for employment purposes
- It is **not exclusive** — the guild's knowledge is open to everyone regardless of recognition level
- It does **not expire**, though active contribution is valued over historical status

The purpose of recognition is to make visible the people who are actively building the shared knowledge and practice of this craft.
