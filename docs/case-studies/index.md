Case studies document real-world examples of Systems Weaving practice. They make the craft visible by describing specific situations, the challenges involved, and the approaches taken.

## Why case studies matter

Patterns describe recurring situations in the abstract. Case studies ground them in real experience. They help practitioners:

- Recognise similar situations in their own work
- Learn from approaches that worked (and those that did not)
- Articulate the value of cross-boundary work to colleagues and organisations
- Build the evidence base for this craft as a recognised discipline

## Submitting a case study

We welcome case study contributions from practitioners at any level. Case studies may be:

- **Anonymised** — details changed to protect organisations and individuals
- **Generalised** — drawn from composite experience rather than a single project
- **Specific** — describing a particular engagement with appropriate permissions

Use the [case study template](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/blob/main/templates/case-study-template.md) as a starting point, and see the [contribution guide](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md) for how to submit.

You can also [open an issue](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/issues/new?template=case-study.yml) to discuss a case study idea before writing it up.
