The Guild of Systems Weavers is open to anyone who practises, studies, or is interested in cross-boundary infrastructure work. There is no membership fee, no application process, and no gatekeeping.

## Ways to participate

### Read and use the resources

All guild resources are public. You are welcome to use the pattern library, competency framework, and other materials in your own practice — with attribution under the [Apache 2.0 licence](https://www.apache.org/licenses/LICENSE-2.0).

### Contribute a pattern

If you have encountered a recurring situation in cross-boundary work, consider documenting it as a pattern. See the [pattern template](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/blob/main/templates/pattern-template.md) and [contribution guide](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md).

### Submit a case study

Real-world examples of Systems Weaving practice are valuable for making this craft visible. See the [case study template](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/blob/main/templates/case-study-template.md). Case studies can be anonymised.

### Improve existing content

Review, refine, and extend the guild's existing resources. Corrections, clarifications, and improvements are always welcome.

### Suggest a term or definition

If the [role glossary](roles/) is missing a relevant term or could define an existing term more clearly, open an issue.

### Participate in governance

The guild's direction is shaped by its contributors. See the [governance page](governance/) for how decisions are made and how to participate.

## Getting started

1. **Read the [manifesto](manifesto)** to understand the guild's purpose
2. **Explore the [patterns](patterns/)** to see the kind of knowledge we are building
3. **Read the [contribution guide](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md)** for practical guidance on making contributions
4. **Open an issue or pull request** on the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers)

## Who is this for?

You may recognise yourself in this guild if you:

- Work across system, team, or organisational boundaries as a regular part of your role
- Have a career that has moved across different technologies, sectors, or organisations
- Find that your most valuable contribution is often connecting, translating, or stabilising things that were not designed to work together
- Have been called a "glue person", "plumber", "Swiss army knife", or "jack of all trades" — and felt that the label was both accurate and inadequate
- Want professional language and community for the kind of work you do

The guild is equally relevant whether you work in IT infrastructure, enterprise services, digital transformation, research computing, operational change, or any other domain where cross-boundary practice matters.
