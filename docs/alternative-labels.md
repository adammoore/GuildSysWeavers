During the development of this guild, several alternative labels were considered for the practice and its practitioners. This page documents those alternatives and the reasoning behind the choice of "Systems Weaver" as the primary term.

## Labels considered

| Label | Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| **Systems Weaver** (chosen) | Broad, skilled, dignified. Captures both the systems focus and the craft of connection | New — requires explanation |
| **Infrastructure Generalist** | Honest, recognisable | "Generalist" undersells the sophistication |
| **Technical Plumber** | Visceral, accurate, reclaimable | Can undersell the work; carries low-status connotations |
| **Workflow Stitcher** | Emphasises process repair | Too narrow — misses the systems and data dimensions |
| **Boundary Architect** | Implies design authority | "Architect" suggests more formal authority than many practitioners have |
| **Cross-System Practitioner** | Descriptive, accurate | Lacks craft connotation; somewhat clinical |
| **Integration Generalist** | Technically accurate | "Integration" sounds like a product implementation role |

## The choice

"Systems Weaver" was selected because:

1. **It names a craft**, not just a role or skill set
2. **"Systems"** is broad enough to encompass technical, organisational, and human dimensions
3. **"Weaver"** implies skilled work — creating coherent fabric from disparate threads
4. **It is new** — it does not carry the baggage of existing labels that have been co-opted or narrowed

## Internal use of alternative terms

"Technical plumber" remains a useful internal metaphor within the guild — a term of solidarity and honest recognition of the sometimes unglamorous nature of the work. It should not be the primary outward-facing label.

Other terms in the table above are used as needed in specific contexts. See the [role glossary](roles/) for formal definitions of each.
