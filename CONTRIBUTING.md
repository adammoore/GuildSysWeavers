# Contributing to the Guild of Systems Weavers

Thank you for your interest in contributing. This guide explains how to participate and what to expect.

## What we welcome

- **New patterns** — recurring situations in cross-boundary infrastructure work
- **Case studies** — real-world examples of Systems Weaving practice (anonymised where appropriate)
- **Glossary suggestions** — new terms or refinements to existing definitions
- **Competency framework input** — refinements based on practice experience
- **Corrections and improvements** — to any existing content
- **Governance suggestions** — proposals for how the guild should be run

## How to contribute

### Quick contributions

- **Open an issue** to propose an idea, report a problem, or start a discussion
- Use the [issue templates](https://github.com/adamvialsmoore/GuildSysWeavers/issues/new/choose) to help structure your proposal

### Content contributions

1. **Fork the repository** and create a branch for your work
2. **Use the relevant template** from the `templates/` directory
3. **Write your contribution** following the tone and style guidelines below
4. **Submit a pull request** using the PR template

### Small fixes

For typos, broken links, or minor corrections, a pull request with a clear description is sufficient — no issue needed.

## Tone and style

Contributions should be:

- **Clear and accessible** — avoid unnecessary jargon; explain technical terms when first used
- **Practice-grounded** — draw on real experience, not abstract theory
- **Respectful** — of different career paths, sectors, and working contexts
- **Professional** — serious but not bureaucratic; thoughtful but not academic
- **Concise** — say what needs to be said without padding

Avoid:

- Startup cliche language
- Enterprise jargon
- Vendor-specific terminology
- Language that assumes a single sector or technology context

Use British English spelling conventions (e.g. "organisation", "recognise", "practise" as verb).

## Review process

1. A maintainer will review your pull request, usually within a week
2. They may suggest changes for clarity, consistency, or completeness
3. Discussion is welcome — the goal is to arrive at the best version of the contribution
4. Once approved, the contribution will be merged

For significant new content (e.g. a new pattern), it is helpful to open an issue first to discuss the idea before writing the full contribution.

## Code of Conduct

All participants in the Guild of Systems Weavers are expected to follow the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Recognition

Contributions are the basis for [recognition](docs/recognition.md) within the guild. All accepted contributions are attributed to their authors.

## Questions?

If you are unsure whether something is worth contributing, or how to structure it — open an issue and ask. We would rather hear a rough idea than miss a good contribution because someone was unsure of the process.
